Tooth Extraction in Coral Springs, FL

From severe decay and overcrowding to advanced gum disease, we’re here to make tooth extraction a smooth and reassuring experience, prioritizing your comfort and optimal oral health for a confident, beautiful smile.

4 Signs You May Need a Tooth Extraction

Overcrowding

Severe crowding or misalignment might require tooth extraction as part of an orthodontic plan, creating the space needed to align your smile perfectly.

Gum Infection

An infection or abscess around a tooth could signal advanced decay or gum disease, often making extraction the best solution to restore oral health.

Severe Tooth Damage

When a tooth is significantly damaged due to decay, trauma, or infection, extraction might be the best way to prevent further issues.

Persistent Pain

If you’re dealing with an ongoing or intense tooth pain that other treatments can’t resolve, an extraction might be the relief you need to get back to living pain-free.

Untreated tooth decay impacts 1 in 5 adults, often leading to the need for tooth extraction.

Tooth Extractions FAQs

If you have any questions about tooth extractions, we’ve got answers.

Your dentist will apply a local anesthetic to numb the area around the tooth. Using precision tools, they’ll gently remove the damaged or infected tooth. After the extraction, you’ll receive detailed aftercare instructions to support a smooth and comfortable healing process.

We prioritize every measure to make tooth extractions as comfortable and pain-free as possible for our patients. While some mild discomfort can occur during recovery, most patients experience minimal to no pain during the procedure.

The time needed for a tooth extraction can vary based on the procedure’s complexity and the patient’s needs. However, most extractions are typically completed in under an hour.

The recovery time can differ based on several factors, including the procedure’s complexity, the patient’s age, overall health, and adherence to post-care instructions. It’s common to feel some discomfort, experience swelling, and notice minor bleeding during the first few days after the procedure. Typically, most patients make a complete recovery within one to two weeks.
